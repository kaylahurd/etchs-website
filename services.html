Service 01

Audiometric Testing

Comprehensive hearing conservation testing delivered by CAOHC-certified technicians using industry-leading microprocessor audiometers inside our acoustically treated mobile units — with full audiologist analysis and reporting.

Mobile Testing Units

  • Acoustically treated mobile units containing audiometric sound rooms
  • Attenuates a minimum of 38 dB at 500 Hz
  • Four-channel microprocessor audiometer for test/retest reliability
  • Ensures continuity across multiple plant locations

Certified Technicians

  • CAOHC certified — Council for Accreditation in Occupational Hearing Conservation
  • Training meets ASHA standards
  • Training meets AIHA standards
  • Training meets AIMA, AAIN, and AAOM standards

Equipment Calibration

  • Daily function check using an electro-acoustic ear
  • Acoustic calibration performed every six months
  • Exhaustive calibration performed annually
  • Exceeds OSHA requirements
  • Meets ANSI S3-1989 specifications

Analysis & Reporting

  • Validated by an audiologist with minimum Master's Degree education
  • Individually evaluated for probable cause of hearing loss
  • Individual binaural impairment percentage calculated
  • Standard Threshold Shift (STS) calculated per employee
  • Alphabetical employee list with ID, test data, and impairment %
  • Report of employees requiring medical referral
  • Monitored reports separating STS employees
  • Course of action determined by audiologist judgment

Audiology Staff & Legal Support

  • Up-to-date files on each state's Workers' Compensation rules
  • Covers noise-induced hearing loss regulations per state
  • Consultation services for any claims filed
  • Legal representation support for Workers' Compensation cases

Also Available

  • Occupational Hearing Conservation Course for in-house testing
  • Employee education programs on hearing protection
  • Noise surveys for workplace assessment
  • Hearing conservation software solutions

Service 02

Chest X-Ray

Full-size onsite chest radiography for the early detection of occupational lung disease. Films are developed immediately, allowing for immediate retakes when necessary.

Overview

Chest X-rays are a valuable tool for demonstrating an anatomical picture of changes taking place within the thoracic cavity — specifically the heart, lungs, and bony structures. Combined with spirometry testing, chest X-rays provide the most effective method for detecting occupational lung disease.

Detection Capabilities

  • Detection of pneumoconiosis (occupational dust diseases)
  • Pleural thickening and/or pleural plaques
  • Early detection of tuberculosis
  • Early detection of carcinoma (lung cancer)
  • Best results when combined with spirometry testing

Quality Assurance

  • Full-size 14×17 chest film for complete imaging
  • X-rays developed immediately on-site
  • Immediate retake capability when needed
  • Comparison reading of previous X-rays available
Clinical Note: Detection of occupational lung disease such as pneumoconiosis is most effective when both chest X-ray and spirometry testing are used together. ETC recommends combining these services for the most comprehensive pulmonary evaluation.
Service 03

Spirometry Testing, Respirator Clearance & Fit Testing

Comprehensive pulmonary function testing by NIOSH-approved RN/technicians using Puritan Bennett PB100 spirometers, with full respirator clearance and qualitative fit testing.

Spirometry Testing

  • Performed with Puritan Bennett PB100 spirometers
  • Conducted by NIOSH-approved RN/Technicians
  • Detection of occupational lung disease
  • OSHA History and Medical Questionnaire — annual interval history thereafter
  • Vital signs including blood pressure and pulse
  • Physical measurements: height and weight

Analysis & Reporting

  • Respirator clearance based on history, spirogram, and vital signs
  • Determined by a Pulmonologist or licensed health care provider
  • Individual annual percent decline calculated per employee
  • Trending data to track pulmonary health over time

Qualitative Respirator Fit Testing

  • Proper use and care training provided at time of fit testing
  • Reports advising employer of each employee's size requirements
  • Identification of special needs or considerations per employee
  • Meets OSHA respiratory protection standard requirements
